A Woman in the Mirror, a film by Tatyana Bronstein

"It is amazing what goes into making something effortless."
Short synopsis:

A woman of two worlds, born of a world of perfection, elegance, beauty and discipline, brings her style and grace to a group of disparate ballet students. Bound by the constraints of our modern, “everyone gets a trophy” society, she shows them the way… a way so different… so unusual… a way to the perfection of effortless motion… “A Woman in the Mirror” .


Short Summary:

“A Woman in the Mirror” is the story of ballerina Alexandra Koltun, who left Russia in search of artistic and personal freedom. On October 15, 1991, while on tour as a soloist with Kirov ballet in Washington DC, Alexandra defected from Russia and relocated to the United States. In 2012 she founded Koltun Ballet Boston, one of the top ballet schools in the country. She teaches as she was taught in her homeland. With flashbacks to her past, tells a story of courage and strength of her character as she leads a culturally and religiously diverse group of young students to their greatest challenge, and tries to provide them that same inner strength that will serve them well throughout their lives. This is a story to be revealed in the studio mirrors that have surrounded Alexandra her entire life.
